# Deflection Phrase

When the interviewee asks "what are you working on?", "what's your product?", or "what are you building?", the interviewer needs a **pre-drafted, verbatim line** to deploy.

## Why pre-draft it

The moment the interviewer ad-libs an answer in the middle of the call, they describe the product. That description anchors every subsequent answer — the customer starts tilting their stories toward whatever framing they just heard. The data is contaminated from that point forward.

The deflection is short, humble, non-specific about the solution, and pivots back to learning. It buys you the whole interview without ever explaining the product.

## Default template

> "I'm so early I have nothing to show yet. Happy to share more on our next call. Today I really just want to learn from you."

Three moves:
1. **"So early"** — signals humility, removes sales pressure.
2. **"Happy to share on our next call"** — preserves the relationship and the future-meeting hook.
3. **"Today I really just want to learn from you"** — redirects back to them.

## Variations by context

**If they've met you before:**
> "Still figuring out what I'm building — I'd rather hear from you today so I can actually get it right. I promise to show you where it lands next time."

**If they push a second time:**
> "Honestly, if I describe it now I'll bias your answers, and then I'll have wasted both our time. Give me another 20 minutes and then I'll share where I am."

**If they're a warm contact and you need to give something:**
Name the **customer segment and problem space**, not the solution. "I'm working on ways to help [segment] with [problem area]" — no product noun, no features. See `vision-statement.md` — same format as the opener.

## What NOT to do

- ❌ "Well, it's an AI billing platform that..." (product noun + features, full contamination)
- ❌ "We're building a variable-pricing model..." (mechanism — primes their answers)
- ❌ "It's kind of like [competitor] but for..." (framing by analogy, primes their answers in a specific direction)
- ❌ Long hedged explanations. The longer the deflection, the more product details leak.

## When to deploy

- Any unprompted "what is this?" question from the customer.
- Any direct ask about the product, pricing, or mechanism.
- When you feel the urge to explain yourself (that urge is the contamination wanting to happen — deploy the deflection instead).

## After the deflection

Immediately pivot back with a question. Don't let silence linger after the deflection — the customer may re-ask. Your next question is ready in your script; go there.

Example:
> Them: "So what is this thing you're building?"
> You: "I'm so early I have nothing to show yet. Happy to share more on our next call. Today I really just want to learn from you. Speaking of which — you mentioned the AccuLynx purchase earlier. Walk me through what happened after the demo..."

Smooth pivot, no awkward pause, conversation stays on their world.
