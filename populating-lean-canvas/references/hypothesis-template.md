# Hypothesis Template

The forcing function for the three Hypothesis boxes (Customer Behavior, Target Metric, Experiment). Writing a clean hypothesis sentence is what binds the three together and filters weak candidates.

## The template

**"If we [X = Experiment], then [Y% = Target Metric] of customers will [Z = Customer Behavior]."**

- **X / Experiment** — the fast-and-frugal minimum thing you run.
- **Y% / Target Metric** — the pre-committed success threshold. A number, not a range.
- **Z / Customer Behavior** — the observable action that tells you the LOFA is true. Always a verb phrase, always something you can see them do.

If the sentence doesn't land cleanly, at least one of the three boxes isn't right. Re-scope.

## How the template filters candidate behaviors

The elicited example from the Lean Canvas session — LOFA: "customers will understand our token-based pricing model." Three candidate behaviors were brainstormed. Here's each one tested against the template:

**Candidate A — choose among pricing models.**
"If we put three pricing models in front of CFOs, then 75% will pick the token-based one."
→ Writes cleanly, but conflates understanding with preference. Picking ≠ understanding.
→ Weak signal on the LOFA. Filter out.

**Candidate B — recall prior use.**
"If we ask CFOs whether they've used token-based pricing before, then X% will have examples."
→ The behavior ("will have examples") isn't a behavior — it's a property of the customer's past. Uncontrollable Y%. Doesn't write cleanly.
→ Filter out.

**Candidate C — define the terms unaided.**
"If we put a short token quiz in front of CFOs, then 75% will give us appropriate definitions."
→ Clean sentence. Strong signal (articulation unaided). Writes in one pass.
→ **Winner.**

Use this move any time you have multiple candidates: try each one in the template. The one that lands in one pass with strong signal is the one.

## Worked examples across LOFA types

### Pricing LOFA

*LOFA: customers will pay $500/mo for the starter tier.*
- If we offer CFOs a paid LOI with a 30-day refund, then 40% will sign and pay the first month.

### Channel LOFA

*LOFA: we can reach mid-size-org CFOs via Vistage groups.*
- If we present at one Vistage meeting, then 3 out of ~12 CFOs in attendance will book a follow-up call.

### Segment LOFA

*LOFA: roofing companies feel the sales-capture pain more than HVAC companies.*
- If we show identical pain-discovery decks to 3 roofing CFOs and 3 HVAC CFOs, then >2/3 of roofing CFOs but <1/3 of HVAC CFOs will self-describe the pain as "top 3" for their business.

### Problem LOFA

*LOFA: salespeople actually skip CRM entries in field sales.*
- If we shadow 3 roofing sales reps for a full day, then fewer than 40% of in-person house visits will produce a CRM entry within 24 hours.

### Solution LOFA

*LOFA: a mobile voice-log app would fix the CRM-capture gap.*
- If we wizard-of-oz a voice-to-CRM app for 3 sales reps over a week, then 75% of their house visits will produce a CRM entry within 1 hour.

## Red flags in the sentence

- **Z isn't a verb phrase.** "Will prefer our solution" — preference isn't a behavior. Rewrite as "will book a demo" or "will sign an LOI."
- **Z involves "feel" or "think" or "believe."** These are SAY, not DO. Rewrite to surface an action.
- **X is vague.** "Put our product in front of them" isn't an experiment; it's a wish. Specify the artifact and the channel.
- **Y% is a range.** "25–50%" is two hypotheses. Pick one. If the team is stuck, pick the higher number and iterate down.
- **The sentence needs a comma splice to work.** If you have to say "and also..." or "if they seem to..." you're stacking hypotheses. One LOFA, one sentence, one cycle.
- **Z depends on X being interpreted correctly.** "Will choose the right option" — right according to whom? Make the behavior objectively observable.

## The target-metric pre-commitment

The Y% is the part teams are most tempted to fudge. Keep in mind:

- On cycle 1, Y% is an educated guess. Team-poll heuristic (optimistic → higher, mixed → lower) or solo 3-factor check (your confidence, prior evidence, signal strength of Z).
- On cycles 2+, Y% is informed by the prior baseline.
- The number's job is to create a line that separates "passed" from "didn't pass" *before* data lands. If the team is still debating Y% after 10 minutes, pick and move on.
- Don't confuse "what we hope happens" with "what we'll call success." The hope can be 95%; the success threshold can be 50%.
